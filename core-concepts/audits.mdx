Every successfully verified step writes an **immutable, hash-chained** audit record.
Sandbox and production records are also publicly signed; when trusted timestamping
is enabled, the RFC 3161 outcome is explicit. The record preserves the material
needed to verify the wallet proof at the applicable disclosure tier. How it is built is covered in
[Verification & trust](/core-concepts/verification#audit-ledger). This page is about
**getting at it**: the id, who can see what, and the lawful path to the full record.

## The audit id

Each record has a stable id of the form `{session_id}_{stage}` — e.g.
`sess_abc123_0` for the first step of `sess_abc123`. You receive it in two places:

- On every [`step.completed`](/api-reference/webhooks#step-completed) webhook, as `audit_id`.
- On every result in [`GET /v1/sessions/{id}`](/api-reference/sessions/get).

Persist the `audit_id` alongside your own `client_reference_id` so you can pull the
record back on demand.

## Two tiers of disclosure

The same record is disclosed at **two very different levels**, by design.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Business — authorized" icon="code">
    What the owning business can fetch with its API key: the proof, chain, timestamp
    status, UIP signature, and encrypted blocks needed to reproduce the record hash.
    `identify`, `sign`, and `light_sign` also disclose their signer and any captured
    wallet evidence. `age_verify` remains redacted to its boolean and threshold.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Legal — full" icon="scale-balanced">
    The complete, **decrypted** record, including signer and wallet evidence for an
    `age_verify` step. It is reached only through UIP's internal, role-gated legal
    workflow after review — not with a customer API key.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

This split is deliberate. A business can re-verify the wallet material for ordinary
disclosures and signatures. An age check stays anonymous to that business because its
DeviceResponse includes the identity used to compute the threshold. All sensitive
blocks remain encrypted at rest.

## Fetch the business record

Call [`GET /v1/audits/{id}`](/api-reference/audits/get) with your API key. It is
scoped to your active business. The response includes the canonical hash context,
timestamp status/token, and sealed ciphertext; sandbox/production records also carry
`uip_signature`. For non-age primitives it includes decrypted `signer` and, for a
wallet-backed completion, `wallet_evidence`.

<Note>
  `proof.claims_digest` commits an `identify` record to the requested claims. The
  returned `signer` lets you recompute that digest. For `sign` and `light_sign`, use
  `wallet_evidence` with the offline verifier to re-run the ISO mdoc proof instead of
  treating the compact `proof.signature` string as sufficient on its own.
</Note>

## Immutability and retention

Completed results are not customer-deletable. Neither account closure nor a
self-service request can alter or remove a result, original completed-sign document,
signature, audit row, encrypted evidence, or hash-chain link. Only artifacts
explicitly classified as transient because no sign result completed may enter
scheduled cleanup. The contractual evidence period and legal-hold process must be set
in the signed customer schedule before production use; expiry of a minimum period is
not an automatic deletion right.

## Legal requests

When a court order, subpoena, or other lawful proceeding requires the **full,
decrypted** record — the attested identity behind a verification — that disclosure
runs through a dedicated, human-reviewed channel, never a customer-facing API.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit a request">
    The requester files at [uip.digital/legal](https://uip.digital/legal) with
    the audit id(s), the matter reference, and the legal basis for disclosure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review">
    UIP's authorized staff review the request against the proceeding.
    Submitting a request does not by itself compel disclosure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disclosure">
    Authorized staff fetch the decrypted record for review, compose the approved
    disclosure, and send the response by email. The decrypted record is not persisted
    in the admin console.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  A legal request does not guarantee disclosure or any legal outcome. UIP reviews
  the requester's asserted identity, authority, and basis, then records the decision.
  Customers remain responsible for their own copies, notices, and legal obligations.
</Tip>
